Who I Am

Chancellor Edwards — known to most as Chance Edwards — is an Application Developer and Product Manager based in Houston, Texas. With a career spanning enterprise software development, government technology, and logistics systems, Chance brings a disciplined, first-principles approach to building software that works.

Chance Edwards earned a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology from the University of the Cumberlands, where he served as Secretary of the Cyber Club and played collegiate basketball. He began his career at IBM, where over three years he contributed to mission-critical applications for the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs and built logistics software systems for UPS.

At the VA, Chancellor automated a business-critical process that cleared a significant backlog, coordinated development across four application components, and led agile ceremonies including sprint planning, retrospectives, and backlog refinement. At UPS, he built and enhanced logistics software while coaching development teams on Scrum best practices.

After IBM, Chance Edwards served as a Product Manager at Sanmore Investments, leading product strategy across the full lifecycle — orchestrating cloud engineering, application development, and design teams. He currently works as an Application Developer at Baca Stein White and Associates, developing enterprise web applications and contributing to data engineering operations.

Chancellor Edwards holds a SAFe Scrum Master certification and certifications in Design Thinking and HTML Development. His technical skill set spans object-oriented programming, web development, SQL, networking, and hardware systems.
